## Section 1 — simracingcoach.com SRC catalog

SimRacingCoach (Spain) sells their own "SRC" line of USB and Bluetooth-wireless steering wheels and panels. All wheels share the same control hardware: 8 push buttons w/ aluminum guards, CNC aluminum magnetic paddle shifters with carbon fiber paddles, 1 Leo Bodnar CTS288V rotary encoder w/ push, 1 7-way joystick, fiber carbon panel, "Extended Mode" yielding 36 inputs without extra software, optional 8-LED RGB module (SimHub), optional dual clutch (+€99), 3-year warranty. PRO variants add a Leo Bodnar SLI-PRO display module. Wireless variants use a TP-Link UB400 Bluetooth dongle, ~50 hr battery, front LED battery indicator.

The SRC store also sells the **VRS DFP20 wheelbase** (€650) — wheelbase, EXCLUDED from this list.

## Section 2 — Missing brands sweep

### Trak Racer (own-brand wheels)

Trak Racer's catalog is overwhelmingly resold third-party wheels (Cube Controls, Simagic, Simucube). Their **own** wheel offering currently visible is a single passive 330mm round rim ("Trak Rally Steering Wheel") — no electronics, intended to mount on someone else's hub. Not a USB box. Excluded — see Notes. Their Alpine Racing TRX line refers to **cockpits**, not wheels.

### SimGrade

"SimGrade" (simgrade.fi) is a **Finnish reseller** carrying Ascher Racing B16M-USB / B16L-USB and others — not a manufacturer of new wheels. **All products already covered** by existing Ascher Racing entries in the guide. Skip.

### Symtec / SymProjects

symprojects.com sells display modules, gauges, LED controllers, joystick controllers — **no steering wheels, no button plates/boxes**. Out of scope. Skip.

### Logitech G PRO Racing Wheel — standalone-USB rim parts

Logitech's **GT D Rim** (and other PRO-line rims) is sold separately but is **not a USB device**: it requires the G PRO Racing wheelbase via Logitech's proprietary quick release. There is no "USB-only" rim from Logitech G. **Excluded.**


### Thrustmaster T818 / T-LCM USB-only rims

Thrustmaster's TQR ecosystem requires a Thrustmaster wheelbase (T818/T598). The Quick Release Upgrade (€29.99) converts existing TM rims to TQR but rims still need a TM base. There are **no USB-direct Thrustmaster rims**. The Sparco R383 add-on is also TM-base only.  **Excluded.**

```js
// EXCLUDED — All Thrustmaster rims require T818/T598/wheelbase. No USB-direct rim sold.
// Quick Release Upgrade kit: €29.99 (https://shop.thrustmaster.com/en_eu/quick-release-upgrade.html)
```

### iSpeedy / Speedmaster

## Notes

### Brands explicitly checked and found nothing new in
- **SimXperience / GS-Cobra** — wheelbase + passive AccuForce rim brand. No standalone USB wheel.
- **SimGrade (simgrade.fi)** — reseller of Ascher Racing (already in guide).
- **SymProjects / Symtec** — display/gauge modules only, no wheels.
- **Logitech G PRO** — rims are proprietary-QR only; no standalone USB rim sold.
- **Thrustmaster (T818, T-LCM)** — all rims require TM wheelbase; no USB-direct rim.
- **Sparco (sparco-official.com)** — gaming line is cockpits/seats only; rim collabs are Thrustmaster or Cube Controls (both already represented).
- **iSpeedy / Speedmaster (Genius)** — entry-level consumer FFB wheel, not a sim USB box.
- **Simjack** — no current Simjack-branded products found via 2026 search.
- **Trak Racer** — sells third-party brands; own-brand offering is a passive 330mm rim ($79, no electronics).
- **3DRap Vintage Collection** — wheels are passive rims only (require external hub electronics); not USB boxes.

### Brands that are wheelbase-only (excluded from scope)
- VRS DFP20 (sold by SimRacingCoach as base)
- SimXperience AccuForce
- Frex WheelBase PRO (Frex's own DD base, separate from their Type-C V2 wheel)
- Fanatec ClubSport Universal Hub V2 — borderline; requires Fanatec base (USB device but proprietary connector). Listed for completeness in "Adapter/Hub" subsection.

### Brands that 404'd and need follow-up
- `https://trakracer.com/collections/sim-racing-steering-wheels` → 404. Correct path is `/collections/steering-wheels-bases` (which is mostly resold brands).
- `https://logitechg.com/en-us/shop/c/racing-accessories.html` → 404. Direct product page works (`/shop/p/pro-gtd-rim`).
- `https://thrustmaster.com/en-us/category/racing/` → 404. `/en-us/universe/racing/` works.
- `https://www.fanatec.com/us-en/steering-wheels/universal-hub` → 404. Use direct product URLs at `/us/en/p/sim-racing-accessories/...`.
- `https://simrep.engineering/...` → DNS / connection failures from this host (try `simrep-engineering.com` or `shop.simrep-engineering.com` — both work).
- `https://3drap.com` → migrating from `3drap.it` to `sim.3drap.it` to `3drap.com` (multi-redirect chain).
- **Frex Type-C V2** — Japanese-only spec sheet; need follow-up to confirm button count, USB vs Frex-proprietary, before listing.

### Recommended additions to the guide (high confidence)
1. **All 13 SRC (SimRacingCoach) wheels and panels** above — directly competes with Ascher / SRB / SimCore plate offerings; covers EUR market gap.
2. **Cosworth CCW Mk2 Pro Sim** — only legitimate IndyCar replica that's a real USB device; halo product.
3. **3DRap VOTA Racing Wheel (Ignition USB)** — only 3DRap finished USB wheel that's not a passive rim.

### Recommended skip / borderline
- **3DRap ROGER Truck Wheel** — niche (truck only).
- **Fanatec Universal Hub V2** — not standalone USB; only useful in Fanatec ecosystem.
- **SIMSONN** — Amazon-only, Logi/TM rim adapter; not relevant to DD-base audience.
- **Frex Type-C V2** — needs spec confirmation before listing.
